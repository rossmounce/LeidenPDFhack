Hieracium sudetotubulosum (Asteraceae), a new name for the illegitimate H.
tubulosum (Tausch) Tausch
ZBIGNIEW SZELĄG
Institute of Botany, Jagiellonian University, Kopernika 31, PL-31-501 Kraków, Poland, azszelag@wp.pl

Hieracium alpinum var. tubulosum Tausch (1828: 63) was raised to species rank as H. tubulosum (Tausch) Tausch
(1837: 68), an illegitimate homonym of H. tubulosum Lamarck (1786: 367). The latter is a different, unrelated
species pertaining to the H. intybaceum aggregate and described from the Alps, whereas Tausch’s taxon is endemic
to the Sudetes and belongs to the H. alpinum aggregate (Chrtek, 1997). Hieracium tubulosum (Tausch) Tausch
differs from other central European species of the H. alpinum aggregate by having tubular florets, so that the
replacement name proposed below is etymologically appropriate.
In his taxonomic revision of the H. alpinum aggregate in the Sudetes, Chrtek (1997: 88) selected the
“lectotypus vel neotypus” of the name H. tubulosum (Tausch) Tausch from among Tausch’s specimens stored at
PRC. I saw that specimen, which obviously is part of the original material and is therefore the lectotype rather than
neotype.
Hieracium sudetotubulosum Szeląg, nom. nov.
Replaced synonym:—Hieracium alpinum var. tubulosum Tausch in Flora 11(1), Ergänzungsbl. 1: 63. 1828 ≡ H.
tubulosum (Tausch) Tausch (1837: 68), non Lamarck (1786: 367).
Lectotype (designated by Chrtek, 1997):—[Czech Republic] In Sudetis, Tausch (PRC!).
Illustrations:—Chrtek (1997: 89, fig. 6; 2004: 553, fig. 4).
